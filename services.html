What We Offer

Comprehensive Care for the Whole Child

We don't provide one service — we provide a life. School, health, relationships, fun, stability, and a real home. All in one place, all built around what each boy actually needs.

Our Programs

Six Areas of Care

Each service area is designed to work together — so that a boy placed with us receives complete, coordinated care from day one.

01

Residential Living

A warm, structured home environment with private bedrooms, family-style meals, consistent routines, and caregivers who show up the same way every day.

Private, age-appropriate bedroom
Nutritious home-cooked meals daily
Consistent daily routines and structure
24/7 trained staff supervision
02

Education Continuity

Every boy attends neighborhood public school — transportation provided. We coordinate with IEP teams, communicate with school counselors, and support academics after school.

Daily transportation to/from school
IEP coordination and support
Daily homework assistance
School counselor communication
03

Medication Administration

We are licensed to administer medications. Strict documentation, physician coordination, and trained protocols ensure every prescription is managed safely and consistently.

Licensed medication administration
Meticulous documentation and logs
Coordination with treating physicians
Pharmacy and refill management
04

Normalcy Activities

Sports, arts, life skills workshops, and community outings are built into every week. Because childhood matters — and no boy in our care should miss out on what makes being a kid meaningful.

Organized sports and outdoor play
Arts, crafts, and creative expression
Life skills development
Community outings and events
05

Family Engagement

Family connections are preserved and strengthened throughout a child's placement. Regular visits, consistent communication, and transition planning keep families involved every step of the way.

Facilitated family visits
Regular family progress updates
Phone/communication access
Transition and reunification planning
06

Case Management Coordination

We work closely with DFPS, courts, attorneys, and referring agencies. Detailed documentation, progress reports, and incident records are maintained to support every legal and administrative requirement.

DFPS/CPS coordination and reporting
Court-ready documentation
Monthly progress reports
Multi-agency communication
Life at Care Option

A Day in the Life

Every day at Care Option is purposeful, structured, and — most importantly — as close to normal as possible.

Morning · 6:30 – 8:00 AM

Wake Up, Breakfast & Getting Ready

Boys wake up in their own bedrooms, get ready for school, and share breakfast together. Morning routines build independence, predictability, and a sense of ownership over the day ahead.

School Day · 8:00 AM – 3:00 PM

Local Schools — Just Like Any Other Kid

We provide transportation to and from neighborhood schools. Boys attend their regular school, maintain friendships, and participate in extracurriculars. IEP coordination and school counselor communication are handled as standard care.

Afternoon · 3:30 – 6:00 PM

Snacks, Homework & Activities

After school: a snack, homework time with staff support, then organized activities — basketball, art projects, life skills classes, or outdoor recreation. Afternoons are purposeful and energetic.

Evening · 6:00 – 9:00 PM

Family-Style Dinner, Games & Wind Down

Evenings center on a shared dinner where conversation and connection happen naturally — followed by board games, reading time, and a consistent bedtime routine that signals safety and predictability.

Weekends

Community Outings, Recreation & Family Visits

Weekends bring community outings, recreation programs, and carefully facilitated family visits. Boys stay connected to the world outside — because that's where they'll eventually return, and we keep them ready.

Eligibility

Who We Serve

We are a specialized program designed for a specific population. Knowing whether a boy is the right fit helps us serve everyone better.

Who We Serve

Boys ages 8–15
Referrals from DFPS/CPS (Texas)
Private family placements
Court-ordered placements
School or counselor referrals
Boys requiring medication administration
Boys able to attend regular public school
Boys needing stability after family disruption

Outside Our Scope

We are not a fit for every child — and we are honest about that from the very first call. Our specialization is a strength, not a limitation.

Girls (program is boys-only)
Children under age 8
Youth ages 16 and above
Acute psychiatric crisis requiring inpatient care
Youth requiring secure/locked facility

If we are not the right fit, we will do our best to help identify who is. We take referrals seriously.
