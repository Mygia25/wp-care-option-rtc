## 5. LICENSING & COMPLIANCE

### What Care Option Needs in Texas

#### Option A: General Residential Operation (GRO) License
- Governed by **Texas Administrative Code Chapter 748**
- Issued by **HHSC Child Care Licensing (CCL)**
- For operations providing 24-hour residential care for children
- Most common license type for group homes and residential facilities for youth
- **This is likely what Care Option will operate under**

#### Option B: Residential Treatment Center (RTC) — Higher Level
- Also under Chapter 748, but with additional requirements under **Subchapter W** (Psychiatric Residential Youth Treatment Facility — PRYTF)
- Requires additional policies, clinical staff, and psychiatric oversight
- More appropriate for facilities providing psychiatric treatment

#### Option C: Qualified Residential Treatment Program (QRTP)
- Federal designation under Family First Prevention Services Act
- Requires **accreditation** from CARF or COA
- Must provide 24/7 nursing and clinical staff
- Minimum 6 months aftercare support
- Can serve DFPS children statewide
- As of 2022, Texas had no approved QRTP providers — major opportunity

### Key GRO Requirements (Chapter 748)

#### Staffing Ratios (Waking Hours)
| Condition | Max Children per Caregiver |

| At least 1 child requires treatment services | **5 children** |

#### Training Requirements (from TACFS)
- Initial training hours for all GRO staff
- Ongoing annual training requirements

## 9. REFERRAL SOURCES

### Who Refers Boys to Residential Treatment Centers in Texas?

#### 1. DFPS Caseworkers (Primary Source)
- Texas Department of Family and Protective Services caseworkers make placement referrals
- DFPS contracts with ~300 licensed residential providers
- Caseworkers use the **Common Application for Placement** (now DFPS Form K902-2355)
- **Referral requirements:** Psychological evaluation (within 14 months), psychiatric evaluation (within 6 months), clinical notes (12 months), discharge documents
- **Assessment timeline:** Within 30 days of initial placement
- Residential contract managers serve as liaisons between DFPS field staff and providers

#### 2. Harris County Juvenile Probation Department (HCJPD)
- 3 in-house facilities for youth ages 10-17
- Forensic Evaluation Unit provides psychological/psychiatric assessments
- Court-ordered placements to external RTCs based on judicial disposition
- Transition Services Unit connects youth to community resources post-release

#### 3. Community-Based Care Providers
- **Our Community Our Kids** (OCOK) manages residential services in North Texas
- Harris County residential services list includes 20+ providers
- Providers include: Children's Hope, Grace Manor, Harbor Point, Kids First, Pegasus Schools, Promise Rose, St. Jude's Ranch, VisionQuest, and others

#### 4. Family/Private Referrals
- Families searching online for placement options
- Pediatricians and family physicians
- School counselors and administrators
- Mental health professionals (therapists, psychologists)
- Churches and community organizations

#### 5. Insurance & Medicaid
- Medicaid can cover QRTP placements under Family First Act
- Private insurance may cover behavioral health residential treatment
- Some facilities accept STAR Health (TX Medicaid managed care for foster children)

### How Case Workers Find Facilities
1. **DFPS Provider Network** — Internal database of contracted providers
2. **HHSC Licensing Database** — Public searchable database of licensed facilities
3. **Google Search** — Case workers DO search online when looking for placement options
4. **Word of mouth** — Recommendations from colleagues and supervisors
5. **Provider outreach** — Facilities that proactively contact DFPS regional offices
6. **Directory listings** — Psychology Today, healthcare directories, etc.

### Website Implications for Care Option
- The website MUST be optimized for case workers and referral sources, not just families
- Include: licensing info, capacity, admission criteria, intake process, contact for referrals
- A dedicated **"Referral Professionals"** page or section is critical

### 1. Lead With Margaret's Story

- Normalcy activities = not institutional

Almost no Houston-based competitor serves boys as young as 8. This is Care Option's unique market position. The website should explicitly address: "Most programs don't accept boys under 10. We do."

- Licensing and compliance info front and center
- Capacity and availability clearly stated
- Direct referral phone line / email
- PDF-downloadable facility overview (for case files)

- Margaret's credentials and experience
- Licensing/accreditation information
- Professional photography (or high-quality AI imagery)
