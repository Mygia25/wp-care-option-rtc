Getting Started

Getting Started Is Easier Than You Think

We've designed our admissions process to be clear, efficient, and human. You'll speak with a real person — not a call center — who will guide you through every step without pressure or paperwork overload.

Our Process

4 Steps to Placement

From first phone call to move-in day, most placements can happen within days — not weeks. We know timing matters when a child needs help.

1

Initial Contact

A 15–20 minute conversation with our team. Ask anything. We have time for you.

~20 min
2

Intake Assessment

We review clinical, medical, and school records to ensure we're the right fit for this child.

24–48 hrs
3

Placement Decision

We coordinate with the case worker, finalize authorizations, and complete required paperwork.

1–3 days
4

Move-In & Transition

His space is ready. The first day is calm, unhurried, and designed to reduce anxiety.

Day of arrival
01
Initial Contact

A Real Conversation, Not a Screening

Call us at (281) 809-3653 or send an email to careoptionrtc@gmail.com. You'll speak directly with a member of our team — not a call center or intake hotline. We want to understand the child's situation, answer your questions, and give you an honest picture of whether we're a good fit.

Explain what's happening and what the child needs
Ask any questions you have — nothing is too basic
We will let you know quickly if we're the right fit
02
Intake Assessment

Thorough, Fast, and Focused

We review the documentation you provide — clinical history, medical records, school records, behavioral assessments — to understand the child's needs and confirm we can serve them well. This is not a gatekeeping exercise; it's how we make sure we're prepared for him on day one.

Clinical and behavioral history review
Medical records and medication list
School records and IEP (if applicable)
Feedback on fit within 24–48 hours
03
Placement Decision

Coordination Without the Chaos

Once we confirm fit, we work directly with the placing agency, case worker, court representative, or family to finalize all required paperwork. We know how to navigate the system — we won't create delays or ask for things twice.

Direct case worker/agency coordination
Authorization and placement paperwork
Transition plan and first-day logistics
04
Move-In & Transition

His Space Is Ready Before He Arrives

We prepare his bedroom, inform the household, and plan a calm, unhurried first day. Our goal is to reduce anxiety — not create it. Families and case workers receive immediate contact once he's settled and comfortable.

Room prepared with his personal items in mind
Calm, structured first-day orientation
Same-day confirmation call to family/case worker
School enrollment started within first week

Placement calls accepted Monday–Saturday, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Call to Start the Process — (281) 809-3653
What to Prepare

Documentation & What to Bring

The more information you can share early, the faster we can move. Here is what we typically request during intake — don't worry if you don't have everything right away.

Documentation for Intake Review

Current clinical or behavioral assessment report
Medical records and active medication list
Birth certificate and immunization records
School records and current IEP (if applicable)
Legal documentation (court orders, custody paperwork)
DFPS case number and contact info (if applicable)
Emergency contact information
Missing something? Call us first and we'll figure it out together. We won't let paperwork slow down a child who needs help.

What to Bring on Move-In Day

At least 5 days of clothing (we can help with the rest)
Prescription medications in original bottles
Personal hygiene items
Any comfort items (books, stuffed animals, photos)
School supplies and backpack (if available)
Signed admission and authorization forms
Photo ID for accompanying adult
We want his first day to feel safe and familiar. Bring whatever makes him feel at home — and tell us what matters to him so we can be ready.
Who Qualifies

Eligibility Overview

We accept referrals from multiple sources. If you're unsure whether a child qualifies, the fastest answer is a quick phone call.

Age & Gender

Boys ages 8–15. We are a specialized boys-only program. We do not accept girls or youth outside this age range.

Referral Sources

DFPS/CPS, juvenile probation, courts, private families, schools, therapists, and other agencies. All referral types are welcome.

School Readiness

Boys must be able to attend regular public school. We do not operate an on-site school but fully support all local school programs including special education.
