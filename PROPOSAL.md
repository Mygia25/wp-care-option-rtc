## 1. Brand Identity Proposal

### Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Primary | Teal | `#00D1A0` | CTAs, highlights, active states, icon fills |
| Primary Dark | Deep Teal | `#00A880` | Hover states, dark backgrounds, nav bar |
| Secondary | Forest Green | `#004D40` | Section headers, footer background, deep accents |
| Accent Warm | Sunrise Gold | `#FFB347` | Highlight badges, award icons, warmth moments |
| Accent Soft | Sky Blue | `#E0F7FA` | Section backgrounds, card backgrounds, soft contrast |
| Background | Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | Main body background |
| Surface | Warm Off-White | `#F8FBF9` | Card surfaces, alternating sections |
| Text Primary | Charcoal | `#1C1C1C` | Body text, headings |
| Text Secondary | Slate Gray | `#5A6472` | Subtext, captions, descriptions |
| Divider | Soft Mint | `#B2DFDB` | Borders, rule lines |

**Why This Palette Works:**
The teal (#00D1A0) reads as "health + growth" without the coldness of clinical blue. The forest green grounds the brand in stability and trust. The warm gold accent keeps it feeling human and celebratory — not sterile. The off-white backgrounds create an airy, home-like feel versus the all-white that reads as "hospital."

---

### Typography

**Heading Font:** [Nunito](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Nunito) — Bold, Extrabold
- Rounded terminals make it feel approachable and warm
- Strong at large sizes, readable at small sizes
- Suggests care and friendliness without being childish

**Body Font:** [Source Sans 3](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Sans+3) — Regular, SemiBold
- Highly legible on all screen sizes
- Professional and clean — important for case workers and agencies
- Neutral enough to not distract from the content

**Accent / Pull Quotes:** [Playfair Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Playfair+Display) — Italic
- Used sparingly for testimonials, hero sub-text, emotional pull quotes
- Adds a warm, human literary quality

**Type Scale:**
- H1: Nunito Extrabold, 56–72px (desktop) / 36–48px (mobile)
- H2: Nunito Bold, 40–48px / 28–36px
- H3: Nunito Bold, 28–32px / 22–26px
- Body Large: Source Sans 3, 18–20px
- Body: Source Sans 3, 16px
- Caption: Source Sans 3, 13–14px
- Pull Quote: Playfair Display Italic, 22–28px

---

### Visual Style

**Overall Direction:** Warm-Home, Not Clinical
The visual language communicates a loving home environment, not a treatment facility. Think: natural light, real spaces, children thriving, not uniforms or examination rooms.

**Photography Style:**
- Natural, warm lighting (golden hour, soft indoor light — never cold fluorescent)
- Candid, documentary style — moments happening, not posed hospital portraits
- Multicultural boys ages 8–15 — joyful, engaged, natural
- Environments: home kitchen, living room, backyard, school hallway, sports field, art table
- No white coats, no clinical equipment visible, no institutional furniture

**Icon Style:**
- Rounded, filled icons (not sharp/angular/outline)
- 2px corner radius minimum
- Custom teal color fills with light backgrounds
- Lucide Icons or Heroicons (rounded variant)

**Graphic Elements:**
- Soft blob/organic shapes as background accents (teal at 8–12% opacity)
- Subtle wave dividers between sections (SVG, teal/mint)
- Rounded cards with box shadows (0 8px 32px rgba(0,0,0,0.08))
- No sharp borders — use border-radius: 16px–24px on all cards
- Thin teal left-border on blockquotes and testimonials

**What to Avoid:**
- No stock photo "sad child" imagery
- No clipart or generic healthcare icons
- No institutional green or hospital blue
- No walls of text — break up with icons, cards, white space
- No aggressive CTAs — this audience is making emotional decisions

#### RESIDENTIAL CARE — Daily Life

**Section Headline:** What a Day at Care Option Looks Like

**Morning:**
Boys wake up in their own space and follow a consistent morning routine — personal hygiene, a home-cooked breakfast, and preparation for school. Mornings are calm, structured, and predictable. For many boys, this routine is the first real stability they've experienced in a long time.

**School Day:**
Our boys attend local public or charter schools in the surrounding area. We handle transportation, communication with school staff, and coordination with IEP and 504 teams when applicable. Homework help and academic support are available every afternoon.

**Afternoons:**
After school, it's structured free time — snacks, outdoor play, sports, or organized activities. We mix individual downtime with group activities to build social skills, teamwork, and the confidence that comes from mastering something new.

**Evenings:**
Family-style dinner around a shared table. Evening activities — board games, TV time, journaling, or quiet reading. Bedtime routines are consistent, calm, and designed to support healthy sleep. Lights out at an appropriate hour based on age.

**Weekends:**
Community outings, recreational activities, special events, and family visits when clinically appropriate and court-approved. Weekends are never dead time — they're intentional investments in normalcy and joy.

---

#### MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION

**Section Headline:** Professional, Licensed, Safe

**Body:**
We are fully licensed to administer medications in a residential care setting. Every medication protocol at Care Option follows strict documentation, dosing accuracy, and safety standards.

Our staff receives ongoing training on medication administration, and we maintain detailed medication logs that are available to case workers, courts, and authorized family members. We coordinate directly with prescribing physicians, psychiatrists, and pharmacies to ensure continuity — especially during transitions.

If a boy arrives with an existing prescription, we work immediately to establish pharmacy relationships and ensure there is no gap in his medical care from day one.

**What This Means for You:**
- No interruption in medication upon admission
- Licensed, trained staff only administer medications
- Detailed documentation for all legal and clinical records
- Direct coordination with your healthcare team

---

#### EDUCATION CONTINUITY

**Section Headline:** His Education Doesn't Stop Here

**Body:**
Residential placement doesn't have to mean falling behind. At Care Option, we make school attendance a priority from the moment a boy is placed with us — not an afterthought.

**How It Works:**
1. **Enrollment Coordination:** Upon admission, we immediately contact the assigned school district and facilitate enrollment or re-enrollment in the appropriate school.
2. **IEP & 504 Advocacy:** If a boy has an active Individualized Education Plan or 504 accommodation, we communicate with the school's special education team to ensure those plans travel with him.
3. **Daily Transportation:** We provide or coordinate reliable transportation to and from school every day.
4. **Homework Support:** Staff are available every afternoon to assist with homework, study skills, and reading support.
5. **Progress Monitoring:** We stay in communication with teachers and school counselors, and we share academic progress reports with case workers and authorized family members.

**Our Goal:**
For every boy to leave Care Option at or above the academic level he was at when he arrived — and ideally, ahead.

---

#### NORMALCY ACTIVITIES

**Section Headline:** Because Being a Kid Still Matters

**Body:**
Research in child welfare consistently shows that maintaining normal childhood activities — sports, arts, friendship, and community — dramatically improves long-term outcomes for children in residential care. We've built this understanding into every week.

**Weekly Activities Include:**

**Sports & Physical Activity:**
Outdoor sports, gym time, and structured physical activities multiple times per week. Whether it's basketball, soccer, swimming, or a neighborhood walk — movement is non-negotiable. Physical activity builds confidence, burns energy healthily, and creates natural bonding moments between boys and staff.

**Arts & Creative Expression:**
Drawing, music, creative writing, and hands-on projects give boys a channel for expression that doesn't require words. Creative activities are especially powerful for boys who are still processing what they've been through.

**Life Skills:**
Cooking simple meals. Doing laundry. Managing a basic schedule. Conflict resolution. Money awareness. These aren't "extras" — they're the practical tools boys will use every day for the rest of their lives, and we build them in naturally alongside daily routines.

**Community Outings:**
Movie nights, bowling, community parks, library visits, museum trips, and local events keep our boys connected to the wider world. Community integration is part of how they learn to navigate life with confidence.

**Family-Style Celebrations:**
Birthdays are celebrated. Achievements are recognized. Progress is cheered. We create moments of joy and belonging because every boy deserves to feel like what he does matters.

**Icon:** Door / home
We prepare a boy's space before he arrives. His first day is designed to be calm, welcoming, and unhurried. We introduce him to the home, meet his housemates, walk through the daily routine, and give him time to settle. We immediately establish communication with his case worker and family contact.

#### WHO WE SERVE

**Section Headline:** Is Care Option Right for Your Child?

**Eligibility:**
- Boys ages 8–15
- Need for structured residential placement as determined by a case worker, court, or child welfare agency
- Referrals accepted from: DFPS/CPS, private case workers, families, courts, school districts, and healthcare providers
- Boys with behavioral health challenges, trauma histories, or developmental needs who can function in a structured, community-based home environment
- Current medication protocols are not a barrier — we are licensed to administer medications

- Boys requiring acute inpatient psychiatric stabilization (we recommend appropriate psychiatric hospital placement first, with transition to Care Option afterward)
- Girls (our program currently serves boys only)
- Boys age 16 or older (we can provide referrals to programs that serve older adolescents)

**Questions About Fit?**
Call us at (281) 809-3653. We'll talk through the specifics and help you determine whether Care Option is the right placement — or point you in the right direction if it isn't. Our goal is the child's wellbeing, not just our census.

Photography is airy and sunlit — indoor and outdoor home environments. Typography is Nunito throughout — rounded, approachable. Cards have gentle shadows and 20px border-radius. Section transitions use subtle wave SVGs.

**Feel:** A well-run foster family's beautiful home. Professional enough for a case worker, warm enough for a mom.

**Best For:** General audience (families + case workers). Maximum accessibility and readability. Most universally trustworthy.

---

### Mood Option B — "Green Growth"

**Aesthetic:** More depth and richness. Deep forest green (#004D40) used for the navigation bar, section headers, and footer. Teal accents pop against the dark green. Content sections alternate between white and a very light mint (#F0FAF7). Hero section features a full-bleed dark green overlay on a warm photography image.

**Feel:** Established, mature, and serious about what it does — while remaining warm. Would feel at home in an upscale healthcare brochure or a premium nonprofit's site.

**Best For:** Agencies and case workers as primary audience. Signals deeper professionalism and longer operational history.

---

### Mood Option C — "Neighborhood Warmth"

**Aesthetic:** Warmer palette, with the sunrise gold (#FFB347) playing a stronger supporting role alongside the teal. More photography-forward design — large images, minimal text blocks. Rounded shapes, organic blobs, playful (but not childish) section breaks. More informal in tone.

**Feel:** Like a neighborhood family that happens to run a beautiful, professional home. Less corporate, more community-based.

**Best For:** If the primary marketing channel will be social media or community referrals. Less appropriate if the primary referral source is court/CPS/professional agencies.

---

### RECOMMENDED: Mood Option A — "Bright Home"

**Justification:**
Care Option's referral network spans a wide audience — from anxious parents to professional case workers to courts. Option A successfully speaks to all of them simultaneously. It's warm enough that a parent doesn't feel intimidated, professional enough that a case worker trusts it, and accessible enough for all screen sizes and technical comfort levels.

The deep green of Option B risks reading as "institutional healthcare" on first impression — precisely what the client wants to avoid. Option C, while beautiful, could undermine credibility with professional referral sources who need to see operational confidence.

Option A is the safest, strongest, and most strategically sound choice for Care Option's specific market.

### Accessibility Requirements

Care Option's audience includes case workers, court-appointed advocates, and social workers — many of whom may use assistive technology. Standards to meet: **WCAG 2.1 AA minimum** (target AAA for color contrast).

**Color Contrast:**
- All body text (#1C1C1C on #FFFFFF): contrast ratio 19.6:1 — exceeds AAA
- Teal CTA (#00D1A0) with white text: verify contrast ratio — may need to darken to #009E7A for AA compliance on buttons
- Recommended button color: #008C6A (white text = 4.9:1, passes AA)

**Keyboard Navigation:**
- All interactive elements (nav, buttons, form fields, links) must be focusable and have visible focus states
- Focus ring: 3px solid #004D40 with 2px offset
- Skip-to-content link at top of page (visually hidden, visible on focus)

**Images:**
- All content images require descriptive alt text
- Decorative images (blobs, dividers) use alt="" (empty alt, not missing)
- Margaret's photo: alt="Margaret Nwajiaku, Founder and Director of Care Option Residential Treatment Center"
- Hero image: alt="Three diverse boys ages 8 to 15 laughing together in a warm, home-like living room"

**Forms:**
- All form fields must have associated `<label>` elements (not just placeholder text)
- Error messages must be announced to screen readers via `aria-live` or `role="alert"`
- Required fields marked with visible asterisk AND `aria-required="true"`
- Dropdown (select) elements must be properly labeled and keyboard-navigable

**Navigation:**
- `aria-label="Main navigation"` on `<nav>` element
- Current page indicated with `aria-current="page"`
- Mobile menu: `aria-expanded` toggles on hamburger button
- All icon-only links must have `aria-label` or visually hidden text

**Semantic HTML:**
- Proper heading hierarchy: one `<h1>` per page, logical H2→H3→H4 flow
- Landmark regions: `<header>`, `<main>`, `<footer>`, `<nav>`, `<section aria-label="...">`
- Phone number link: `<a href="tel:+12818093653">(281) 809-3653</a>`

**Performance (affects accessibility for low-bandwidth users):**
- Target: Lighthouse Performance score 90+
- Images: WebP format, lazy loading except hero (eager)
- Hero image: preload `<link rel="preload">` in `<head>`
- Fonts: `font-display: swap` on all Google Fonts
