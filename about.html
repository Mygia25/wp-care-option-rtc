Who We Are

A Different Kind of Care

Where professional expertise meets a mother's instinct.

Care Option was born from a simple observation: Houston had placements for teenagers, and plenty of options for girls — but the youngest boys, the 8, 9, and 10-year-olds caught in the system, were being placed wherever there was space. Not wherever there was care.

Our founder, Margaret Nwajiaku, spent more than 18 years leading Medicare-certified healthcare agencies. She understands what clinical standards look like — documentation, medication protocols, staff credentialing, regulatory compliance. She also knows what those standards miss: the warmth, the consistency, the sense of home that children need to actually heal.

"The boys who come to us have already lost so much. What they don't need is another clinical setting. What they need is someone who treats their bedroom like their bedroom — not a room number."

Care Option was built to bridge that gap. Licensed in Texas, capable of medication administration, coordinated with local schools — and built to feel like a home, not a facility. Every policy, every protocol, and every hire has been made with that intention.

Our Mission

To provide a safe, nurturing, home-like residential environment where boys ages 8–15 can heal, grow, stay connected to school, and experience the childhood they deserve — supported by healthcare-grade expertise and genuine human care.

Our Vision

A Houston where no child in the residential care system has to choose between safety and normalcy. Where boys ages 8 and up have access to high-quality, home-like care from day one — not as a last resort, but as a first-choice placement.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Every decision we make — from staffing to daily schedules — runs through these four commitments.

Safety

Physical and emotional safety is non-negotiable. Every child in our care is protected around the clock by screened, trained professionals.

Dignity

Every boy in our care is treated with respect, patience, and the presumption that he is capable of growth — regardless of what brought him here.

Continuity

School, relationships, routines — we work to protect and maintain what already exists in a child's life, minimizing disruption and maximizing stability.

Partnership

We work as partners with families, case workers, courts, and schools — sharing information openly, communicating proactively, and treating every stakeholder with professionalism.
